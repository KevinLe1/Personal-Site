Projects



FIRST Tech Challenge Robotics

Over a dozen various awards won from 2014-2016, along with a quadrupling of team funds.

Forsyth Independent Research

In Fall of 2014, a friend and I presented an original research proposal concerning the interactions between M. smegmtatis (closely related to M. tuberculosis ) and C. elegans to the Forsyth Institute's Immunology Department. The following summer, we were invited to conduct our research at the facility with full funding.

Teams



Barnard-Columbia Design for America (DFA)

DFA is a multidisciplinary group focused on human-centered design. I am on a team exploring solar energy solutions for apartment buildings in Harlem by developing portable, modular, and affordable solar power units to promote a transition to solar power purchase agreements.

www.bcdfa.com



Columbia Space Initiative (CSI)

Columbia's undergraduate aerospace club; I am on the Micro-G NeXT team and Rocket team. In Micro-G NeXT, I am part of a small team developing a novel subsurface sampling device for astronaut use on asteroids. Our design was approved by NASA and we are currently in a manufacturing phase. In the Rocket team, we are developing a class-K rocket with experimental engines, for which I am working on the body.

www.columbiaspace.org



Engineers Without Borders - Columbia Chapter

Engineers Without Borders designs and builds infrastructure for developing communities. I am on the water team of the Morocco division. My focus is on designing a water filtration and storage system for Ait Bayoud, a community in Morocco. Physically building it will occur in the summer.

www.morocco.cuewb.org



Columbia Biomedical Engineering Society (BMES)

BMES works to organize social and professional events for students interested in biotech. Within BMES, I am a board member involved with the group's Student Life Committee. I am also one of the primary organizers for an upcoming medical-themed 24-hour hackathon called BioHacks NYC, which will be the first of its kind at Columbia.
