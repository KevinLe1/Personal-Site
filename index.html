Projects



LHS MakerSpace ("OpenSource")

At my former high school (Lexington High School, Lexington, MA), there were almost no engineering supports for students at the school besides the student-run robotics team, which had its own limitations. In response, I established an experimental MakerSpace called "OpenSource" with minor funding from the Lexington Education Foundation (LEF) for students to create whatever, whenever. Since then, it has expanded into the largest engineering education support at LHS with its own space and computer lab along with use both in and out of school by students and faculty.


Elementary Engineering Education Reform

Similar to my high school, the six elementary schools in my town also lacked engineering supports. After running robotics classes for young students independently for over a year, it was clear that there was a demand. I led an initiative to get the school system to invest in new engineering supports and designed original engineering curricula and teacher training materials. After a successful pilot year, the curriculum is becoming fully integrated across the Lexington, MA school system.

Forsyth Independent Research

In Fall of 2014, a friend and I presented an original research proposal concerning the interactions between M. smegmtatis (closely related to M. tuberculosis ) and C. elegans to the Forsyth Institute's Immunology Department. The following summer, we were invited to conduct our research at the facility with full funding.
