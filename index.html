Teams



Engineers Without Borders (Columbia Chapter) - Water Project Lead and Fundraising Chair

Engineers Without Borders designs and builds infrastructure for developing communities. I am responsible for overseeing all aspects of a project to develop and implement a clean water access system for Ait Bayoud in Morocco. Currently, the team is working on sediment filtration, chemical purification, and on constructing a temporary distribution site. The goal is for the aforementioned to be built in Morocco over Summer 2017. As fundraising chair, it is also my responsibility to ensure that we raise enough money to travel and implement the chapter's solutions.

www.morocco.cuewb.org

View an update of our progress as of Winter 2017.



Columbia Space Initiative (CSI) - Micro-G Outreach Lead and Micro-G NeXT Design Member

Columbia's undergraduate aerospace club; I am on the Micro-G NeXT team. In Micro-G NeXT, I am part of a small team developing a novel subsurface sampling device for astronaut use on asteroids which is able to work on a purely mechanical basis (no motors or electricity). The device should be able to take both surface and core samples (modelled as regolith and sandstone, respectively) while maintaining stratification. Our design was approved by NASA and we are currently in a manufacturing phase. As an outreach lead, I also organize numerous events in the community such as aerospace workshops for local community centers.

www.columbiaspace.org

View the project here



Barnard-Columbia Design for America (DFA) - Studio Member

DFA is a multidisciplinary group focused on human-centered design. I am on a subteam exploring ways to address environmental inequity in NYC.
